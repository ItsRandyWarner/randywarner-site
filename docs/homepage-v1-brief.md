# Homepage V1 Brief

## Purpose

Build the first real version of the homepage: a personal creative hub that makes visitors curious, gives them a few meaningful things to explore, and invites them to come back for updates.

This version should not try to implement the full future vision. It should establish the feeling and structure of the site while connecting to the implemented writing and podcast paths.

## Primary Visitor Behavior

Explore.

The homepage should encourage visitors to browse writing, learn about the podcast, notice future creative areas, and feel that the site is alive enough to revisit.

## Content Priorities

1. Writing
   - Short and long-form writing should be visible in the first version.
   - Initial themes: life, relationships, spirituality, health, stories, and comedy.
   - The homepage writing section pulls real posts from the writing collection.

2. Podcast
   - The podcast should have a clear featured section.
   - The podcast is The YaS Cast.
   - RSS feed: `https://feeds.transistor.fm/the-yas-cast-yuba-and-sutter-podcast`
   - The homepage should point visitors toward the implemented `/podcasts` hub for the fuller podcast path.

3. Future Creative Areas
   - Photos, creative projects, and store can be represented as lightweight paths.
   - These should feel like part of the larger world of the site, not unfinished errors.
   - Community and giveaway ideas should not be mentioned in v1.

## Recommended Homepage Sections

1. Hero
   - Randy Warner as the first-viewport identity signal.
   - A short line about creativity, curiosity, connection, and making/sharing things.
   - Primary action: explore.

2. Writing
   - A few real writing cards from the writing collection.
   - Separate labels for short and long-form if useful.

3. Podcast
   - Podcast title, description, and listening path.
   - Link visitors to `/podcasts` for Spotify, Apple Podcasts, artwork, and request form access.

4. Creative Index
   - Small set of paths: photos, projects, shop, and social links.
   - Keep shop intentionally secondary.

5. Return Invitation
   - A simple closing section that tells visitors the site will keep changing.
   - Avoid sales language.

## Tone And Copy Guidance

- Use Randy's voice where possible.
- Placeholder copy is allowed during development.
- Before launch, Randy should review and rewrite prominent copy.
- Avoid professional portfolio language unless a specific section needs it.
- Avoid funnel, course, hustle, or sales language.

## Design Direction

- Creative and personal before polished and professional.
- Experimental is welcome, but should support exploration rather than become a gimmick.
- Use clean alignment, strong composition, thoughtful contrast, color, texture, and pattern.
- Avoid sterile minimalism and plain white-box portfolio energy.
- Chosen direction: start with Eames/Girard order and warmth, add Corita Kent color and typographic spirit, and use only a small amount of David Carson looseness.
- The homepage should feel organized enough to navigate and loose enough to feel alive.
- Visual design should leave room for writing, podcast artwork, photos, and creative project imagery later.
- The hero should move toward a personal image treatment. For now, use a stylized portrait placeholder rather than a purely abstract art poster.
