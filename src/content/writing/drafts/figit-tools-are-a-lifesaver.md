## Quick Frontmatter Notes

- `title`: the title people see on the site.
- `description`: the short preview text used on cards and metadata.
- `date`: the publish date or intended publish date.
- `updated`: only keep this if the piece has been revised after the original date.
- `type`: choose one of these:
  - `essay`: a longer, more developed piece.
  - `note`: a shorter thought, reflection, or observation.
  - `story`: a narrative about something that happened.
  - `list`: a collected set of things, ideas, lessons, links, or recommendations.
- `topics`: remove anything that does not apply.
- `draft`: keep `true` until the piece is ready to publish.
- `featured`: set to `true` only when you want the piece highlighted.
- `slug`: the URL ending, like `my-great-post`.
- `image`: replace this with a real image later, or delete the whole image block.
- `canonicalUrl`: delete this unless the piece was originally published somewhere else or you need a specific canonical URL.

## Markdown Cheat Sheet

Write normal paragraphs with a blank line between them.

Use **bold text** when something needs emphasis.

Use *italic text* for a lighter kind of emphasis.

Use ==highlighted text== when you want the site's yellow highlight style.

Use [link text](https://example.com) to add a link.
